Projects

WADA Solutions

This site began as an idea for a backend programmer, where I envisioned a fitting logo featuring two intersecting curly brackets. I also designed a business card and a calendar, which you can explore in the branding section. This project gave me the opportunity to experiment with new design concepts and to apply my skills in creating cohesive branding materials. Although it was an early effort, I consider it an important milestone in my journey towards mastering design and branding.

Streamer Website

This site was originally intended for a streamer, where I envisioned having all social networks easily accessible. I put new skills into practice here, including creating vector graphics with Inkscape. I experimented with a leather-like surface effect, making the elements appear as if they were sewn onto the fabric. It was clearly an experiment, and there’s definitely room for improvement in the future.

Heat Wave

This was my first real project. Despite many mistakes and little experience, I created a logo (with Paint 3D), chose a color palette, and built the site for a plumbing and heating company. I didn’t even know what a wireframe was back then. This project marks an important step in my learning and reminds me how far I’ve come.

Old Curriculum

This is an old version of my CV. I used Blender to create my name in 3D, incorporated an image of the moon, and made a GIF with GIMP to simulate water. Though it has mistakes and lacks best practices, it marks an important phase in my learning journey. I cherish it as a personal record of growth and a foundation for my current skills.

Glastonbury Festival

This is a bootcamp project where I showcased my web development skills by recreating the Glastonbury Festival homepage with a personal touch to boost engagement. The project includes a detailed PowerPoint presentation, plus wireframes and mockups created in Figma. It demonstrates my ability to blend creative design with technical skills to build an engaging, functional site.
