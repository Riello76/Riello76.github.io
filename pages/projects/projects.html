Old Portfolio

This was my first portfolio, or at least what I envisioned at that time. For example, I experimented with replacing the traditional hamburger menu with more prominent buttons to enable quicker navigation on mobile devices using just one finger. While the sites may lack best practices and are filled with mistakes, they represent an important learning phase in my development journey. This portfolio is a personal album of memories and growth, showcasing my initial steps in web design and development. The approach was driven by a desire to innovate and test new ideas, even if it meant making some missteps along the way. I cherish it as a reminder of how far I've come and the foundation upon which I've built my current skills.

Glastonbury Festival

This is the bootcamp project I attended, where I had the opportunity to showcase my web development skills. For this project, I recreated the Glastonbury Festival home page, adding a unique touch of personification to enhance user engagement. The site features a detailed PowerPoint presentation outlining the project scope, objectives, and development process. Additionally, a comprehensive wireframe and mockup were created on Figma, providing a clear visual representation of the design and layout. This project demonstrates my ability to combine creative design with technical proficiency to deliver an engaging and functional web page.
